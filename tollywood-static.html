Telugu Film Industry

Baahubali 2 : The Conclusion (2017)

Baahubali 2: The Conclusion is an epic Indian film that was released in 2017. Directed by S.S. Rajamouli, it is the second and final installment of the Baahubali franchise. The film continues the story of the ancient kingdom of Mahishmati and follows the protagonist, Mahendra Baahubali (played by Prabhas), as he seeks to avenge the betrayal and death of his father, Amarendra Baahubali. The movie is known for its grandeur, spectacular visual effects, and powerful performances. It explores themes of duty, honor, and vengeance, culminating in a thrilling and emotionally charged climax.

Pushpa 2 : The Rule (2024)

Pushpa 2: The Rule continues the saga of Pushpa Raj, a laborer-turned-red sandalwood smuggler. The film, directed by Sukumar, stars Allu Arjun, Rashmika Mandanna, and Fahadh Faasil. Pushpa faces new threats from his enemies, including SP Bhanwar Singh Shekhawat. The movie delves deeper into Pushpa's rise to power, his personal struggles, and his quest for respect and acceptance. With intense action sequences, emotional drama, and a gripping narrative, Pushpa 2: The Rule captivates audiences and sets the stage for the next installment in the series

RRR (2022)

RRR is a 2022 Indian Telugu-language epic action drama film directed by S. S. Rajamouli. The story reimagines the lives of two Indian revolutionaries, Komaram Bheem (N. T. Rama Rao Jr.) and Alluri Sitarama Raju (Ram Charan), who join forces to fight against British colonial rule in the 1920s. The film features intense action sequences, emotional drama, and a gripping narrative. With a star-studded cast including Ajay Devgn and Alia Bhatt, RRR received critical acclaim for its direction, performances, and visual effects. It became one of the highest-grossing Indian films of all time

Kalki 2898 AD (2024)

Kalki 2898 AD is a 2024 Indian Telugu-language epic mythological science-fiction film directed by Nag Ashwin. Set in a post-apocalyptic world 6000 years after the Kurukshetra War, the film follows Ashwatthama, cursed with immortality, as he protects Sumati, the mother of Vishnu's final avatar, Kalki. Supreme Yaskin, a totalitarian god-king, rules the dystopian city of Kasi from the Complex, an inverted-pyramid megastructure. The film stars Amitabh Bachchan, Kamal Haasan, Prabhas, Deepika Padukone, and Disha Patani. With a ₹600 crore budget, it became one of the highest-grossing Indian films of 2024.

Salaar 1 : Ceasefire (2023)

Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire is a 2023 Indian Telugu-language epic action drama film directed by Prashanth Neel. Set in the fictional dystopian city-state of Khansaar, the film follows the friendship between Deva (Prabhas) and Varadha (Prithviraj Sukumaran). When Varadha's father's ministers and relatives plan a coup d'état to prevent him from becoming king, Varadha enlists Deva's help to secure his rule. The film features intense action sequences and a gripping narrative, with a star-studded cast including Shruti Haasan, Jagapathi Babu, and Bobby Simha.

Baahubali 1 : The Beginning (2015)

Baahubali: The Beginning is a 2015 Indian Telugu-language epic action film directed by S. S. Rajamouli. The story follows Shivudu (Prabhas), a young man with extraordinary strength, who discovers his royal lineage and his connection to the kingdom of Mahishmati. As he learns about his true identity as Amarendra Baahubali's son, he embarks on a journey to reclaim his kingdom from the tyrannical Bhallaladeva (Rana Daggubati). The film features breathtaking action sequences, stunning visual effects, and a gripping narrative. Baahubali: The Beginning became a massive commercial success and set the stage for its sequel.

Saaho (2019)

Saaho is a 2019 Indian action thriller film directed by Sujeeth. The story revolves around two undercover officers, Ashok Chakravarthy (Prabhas) and Amritha Nair (Shraddha Kapoor), who are on a mission to uncover the mastermind behind a ₹2,000 crore heist. As they delve deeper, they realize the case is linked to the death of a prominent business tycoon and an emerging gang war in the fictional Waaji City. The film features intense action sequences, stunning visual effects, and a gripping narrative. Despite mixed reviews, Saaho became one of the highest-grossing Indian films of 2019.

Devara (2024)

Devara: Part 1 is a 2024 Indian Telugu-language action drama film directed by Koratala Siva. The film stars N. T. Rama Rao Jr. in dual roles, alongside Saif Ali Khan, Janhvi Kapoor, Prakash Raj, and Shine Tom Chacko. Set in the coastal village of Ratnagiri, the story follows Devara, a chieftain, who feuds with his counterpart Bhaira over arms smuggling through the Red Sea. The film features intense action sequences, emotional drama, and a gripping narrative. Released on September 27, 2024, it became a commercial success, grossing ₹380–521 crores.

Pushpa 1 : The Rise (2021)

Pushpa 1: The Rise is a 2021 Indian Telugu-language action drama film directed by Sukumar. The story follows Pushpa Raj (Allu Arjun), a laborer who rises through the ranks of a red sandalwood smuggling syndicate in the Seshachalam Hills of Andhra Pradesh. As Pushpa gains power, he faces challenges from his enemies, including DSP Govindappa (Shatru) and rival smuggler Mangalam Sinu (Sunil). The film also explores Pushpa's personal life, including his relationship with Srivalli (Rashmika Mandanna). With intense action sequences, emotional drama, and a gripping narrative, Pushpa: The Rise became a commercial success.

Adipurush (2023)

Adipurush is a 2023 Indian mythological action film directed by Om Raut. Inspired by the Hindu epic Ramayana, the film stars Prabhas as Raghav, Kriti Sanon as Janaki, and Saif Ali Khan as Lankesh. The story follows Raghav, the exiled prince of Ayodhya, on his journey to rescue his wife Janaki from the demon king Lankesh. With a budget of ₹500−700 crore, Adipurush became one of the most expensive Indian films. Despite high expectations, the film received mixed reviews for its screenplay, dialogues, and visuals, and it struggled at the box office.
